Here's a time line of Countess Ada Lovelace

  • Dec 10, 1815 - Born under the name Ada Augusta Byron , she was the only child of the poet George Gordon the 6th (Lord Byron) and his wife, Anne Isabella "Annabella" Milbanke (Baroness Wentworth).
  • Jan 16, 1816 - Ada's parent get separated
  • Apr 19, 1824 - Lord Byron (Ada'a father) dies in a war in greece
  • Jan 1, 1828 - Ada designs a flying
  • Oct 1, 1829 - Ada gets measles and is unable to leave bed
  • Apr 1, 1832 - Recovers from measles, she can walk with crutches.
  • Jan 1, 1833 - Ada meets Charles Babbage. He disscusses he's difference engine with her. She is inspire by his words.
  • Jul 8, 1835 - Ada weds William King
  • May 12, 1836 - Ada's first child is born, Byron
  • Sep 22, 1837 - Ada's second child is born, Anne Isabella
  • Jun 30, 1838 - Ada becomes Countess of Lovelace
  • Jul 2, 1839 - Ada's third child is born, Ralph Gordon
  • Aug 1, 1843 - Ada's translation and Notes on the analytical engine are published. This is her life's achievement; inside is what is consider to by the worlds first computer program.
  • Nov 27, 1852 - Lovelace died at the age of thirty-six from uterine cancer and bloodletting by her physicians. She was buried, at her request, next to her father at the Church of St. Mary Magdalene in Hucknall, Nottingham.
